Restoring a wallet

If your computer is lost, stolen or damaged, or perhaps you've accidentally deleted your MultiBit HD information, then you will need to restore a wallet. This article guides you through this process.

Step 1 - Locate your wallet backups

A wallet backup is an encrypted copy of your Bitcoin wallet that you can use to recover:

  • your bitcoin
  • your contacts and notes
  • your payment history and notes
  • the currencies and exchange rates used in payments

If you followed the instructions in the getting started guide then you will have a cloud backup service that continuously uploads your encrypted wallet files to a server somewhere in the cloud.

If you don't have a cloud backup but you do have your seed phrase then you will be able to recover your bitcoin and any transactions present on the block chain. Unfortunately, all the extra information such as contact details, payment notes, exchange rates and so on are only present in wallet backups and will be lost.

Step 2 - Start the restore process

From the unlock screen click the "Restore" button and follow the instructions given in the restore wizard.

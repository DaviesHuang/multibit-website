Introducing BRIT

What is the best way to provide Bitcoin users with high quality wallet software ? How do the devs get paid ?

This article describes how we plan to do this for MultiBit HD. We call it the Burton-Rowe Income Technique (BRIT).

Overview

With MultiBit HD we are introducing a client fee of 1,000 satoshi per spend. (For comparison: the current minimum miner's fee is 10,000 satoshi). This fee will go towards further development of MultiBit HD. We will also use the funds raised to support upstream projects that MultiBit HD relies upon (such as Bitcoinj, XChange etc). By charging a client fee we can avoid intrusive or privacy-breaking alternatives.

Background

MultiBit has been in the Bitcoin space since 2011. In that time it has seen continuous development and improvement. Recently we celebrated 1.5 million downloads.

Supporting a metropolis of users and writing new features is now a full time job for a team.

Since October 2013 we've been working hard on the next version of MultiBit (MultiBit HD) which has a much better interface and a deterministic wallet.

Like everyone we have bills to pay so we need to monetise MultiBit HD. Here are our guiding principles for creating MultiBit HD:

  • Must be open source under MIT license
  • No gathering or selling of information to third parties
  • No in-app advertising
  • Zero upfront cost
  • Must be decentralised apart from an anonymous initial contact
  • Fees must be extremely low
  • Must allow income to be allocated to other parties efficiently
  • All communications must rely on encrypted messages to prevent snooping, MITM and replay attacks

The result was the creation of a payment system, BRIT, with a client fee of 1,000 satoshi per transaction sent. At $500 per bitcoin this is 0.5 cents for each transaction sent. The price of a cup of coffee in Starbucks will pay for over 500 sends. There is no charge for receiving bitcoin. As this fee is below the Bitcoin dust limit of 5432 satoshi we bundle up these small amounts - you pay for them once every twenty to thirty sends from your wallet.

Technical details

BRIT contains some interesting technical details which will be of use to other developers of open source software. All the payment code, both client and server side, will be released under the MIT licence for other teams to use freely.

tl;dr

To pay for future MultiBit development, we will be introducing a fee of 1,000 satoshi per spend in MultiBit HD.
